Interviewing and Users

I interviewed two users to gather data on what music streaming app they use the most and how they use it. From there, I formed distinct user scenarios that highlighted what was good or bad about the design of the app, citing specific interview points as reference. Shown here is one such annotated user scenario.

Competitive Analysis

After focusing on key elements of several apps, I formed a comprehensive analysis of all individual features of three: Amazon Music, Spotify, and Soundcloud. This helped to see how popular apps structure and organize features that the user reaches through the main navigation bar. Surprisingly, two of three apps balanced features badly by putting way too much in the 'home' section.

From Paper to Axure

By analyzing the structure of the other apps, my group all had opinions about how our redesign should look. Where should playlist settings go? How easy should it be to delete something? We decided to base most decisions off the data from interviews, with one of my interviews proving useful.

Final Product

Using Axure, one teammate brought our hard work into an interactive user scenario. I provided editing for some elements, screenshots of Spotify, and shared ideas about how and where interaction should happen. I helped organize some of our toughest decisions and linked the justification to each one.
